Cocteau Twins

Scottish alternative rock group Cocteau Twins first gained notoriety in the early 1980s. Their sound was characterized by the eerie vocals of Elizabeth Fraser, atmospheric guitars, lush textures, and complex rhythms. They worked with numerous other musicians and put out a number of critically acclaimed albums. Although they broke up in 1997, they continue to have an impact on a variety of musicians and genres, and their music is a testament to the value of experimentation and innovation.

In the 1980s and the early 1990s, The Cocteau Twins were at the height of their popularity, mesmerizing audiences with their ethereal and dreamy soundscapes. Their personal lives and the music they produced frequently reflected each other's romanticism and longing. Many of the band's love songs were inspired by Elizabeth Fraser's long-term relationship with Robin Guthrie, the other band member. Their drug use, which they frequently employed as a creative tool to access their subconscious and improve their artistic expression, had an impact on their music as well. Fraser has talked in particular about her use of LSD and how it enabled her to enter a different state of consciousness and produce some of her most iconic lyrics.
